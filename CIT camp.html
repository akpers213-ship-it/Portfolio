    CIT Camp

    Hands on learning helping you to become an assistant for the children's camps.

Become A Counselor

To become a counselor you must complete at least 2 full weeks of CIT camp and be at least 15 years old. To become a Lead Counselor you must be at least 16 years old

Things You Do...

Students around here  who are willing to learn  a little,  get life lessons that will help mold them into well rounded horse kids who can participate in every aspect of life here during camp season, from feeding horses to taking out the trash to talking to parents.

What You Learn

Our Counselor-in-Training (C.I.T.) program is designed for young people who want to serve and lead, even if they aren't currently taking weekly lessons. This program offers a hands-on look at what it takes to be a counselor at His Barn. What You’ll Do You will shadow our experienced staff and the owner, Ms. Jillian, to learn the "ins and outs" of camp life. Your week will be a mix of: Leadership: Leading Bible studies, crafts, and games. Animal Care: Working in the barn and performing daily chores. Horseback Riding: While not a daily activity, you will ride 2–3 times during the week. Camper Support: Assisting with activities inside the house. Perks & Requirements Food: All lunches and snacks are included (plus extra snacks just for C.I.T.s!). Future Opportunities: Completing two full weeks of C.I.T. camp makes you eligible to apply for volunteer counselor positions. Availability: Spots are very limited each summer, so early registration is encouraged. This is a serious but rewarding step toward becoming a leader. We ask that you consider the commitment carefully before applying. Parents: Please call us with any questions!
